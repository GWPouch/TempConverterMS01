Kelvin

The Kelvin⧉ is the S.I. unit⧉ of temperature named after William Thomson, 1st Baron Kelvin⧉.

An increase in temperature of 1 Kelvin is an increase in temperature of 1°C. The zero-point on the Kelvin temperature scale is Absolute Zero ⧉. Water freezes at 273.15K.

Strictly speaking, a kelvin is a unit like a meter or ampere; a temperature is a certain number of kelvins (note lower-case), not Degrees Kelvin, as in "Water freezes at 273 kelvins"
